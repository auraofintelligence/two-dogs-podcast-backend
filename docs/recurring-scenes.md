# Show Rhythm And Recurring Bits

Status: working menu
Updated: 2026-06-21

## Purpose

This is the repeatable rhythm shelf for The Two Dogs Podcast.

Use it when a yarn needs a new angle, a current-event doorway, a laugh, a source check, a warning, a win, a cutaway, a practical tip or a way back to dog logic.

These are prompts, not scripts. Angel directs Blue Dog. Luke directs Red Dog. Guests choose their own creature lens.

## Useful Habits

- Current events, weather, sport, public claims and Bad Dog items are better with a fresh source check on recording day.
- Good Dog works best when it names a real action, not just a nice feeling.
- Bad Dog works best when it points to process, risk, bureaucracy, bad incentives or documented choices rather than loose pile-ons.
- Comedy Minute is there to release pressure, not to make someone smaller.
- The strongest bits usually leave a question open.

## Regular Scene Menu

### Dog News / News Flash

A current headline that might become Good Dog, Bad Dog, Famous Dogs, Dog Rituals, News Flash or nothing at all.

Red Dog angle: what happened, what is known, and what is worth watching next?

### Comedy Minute

A quick absurd translation, visual gag or pressure release before the dense idea comes back.

Red Dog angle: what is the plain-language ridiculous version?

### Bad Dog

For corruption, bad choices, avoidable risk, poor process, scammy behaviour, public-interest failures or "what were they thinking?" moments.

Red Dog angle: what went wrong, what got put at risk, and what would a better system have noticed earlier?

### Good Dog

For good news, useful effort, community care, local wins, scientific breakthroughs, practical repairs and people giving life a fair go.

Red Dog angle: what did someone actually do?

### UN World Day Of Whatever

An official observance can become a strange doorway into the topic.

Red Dog angle: apparently today is World Something Day, and weirdly it might help us look at this from another angle.

Reference: https://www.un.org/en/observances/list-days-weeks

### Sports Desk

Sport as ritual, rivalry, fairness, training, mascots, community health and joyful nonsense.

Red Dog angle: what did the game teach us about people?

### Weather Window

Island weather, storms, heat, tides, ferries, outdoor plans and humility from the sky.

Red Dog angle: is the weather part of the story today?

### Music Drop

Theme-song cues, local tracks, release memory, guest songs, playlists and soundtrack beds.

Red Dog angle: what feeling does this sound carry into the scene?

### Film Club

Films, shorts, animation tests, outdoor cinema, scene studies and Strange But True film-club links.

Red Dog angle: what would this look like as a scene?

### Art Show

Exhibitions, posters, visual styles, props, murals, set dressing and guest character aesthetics.

Red Dog angle: what image makes the idea easier to feel?

### Games Table

Tabletop ideas, public ledger games, simulation toys, audience prompts and playful decision tests.

Red Dog angle: can we make the system small enough to play with?

### Dogs And Aliens

Sci-fi creatures, UAP headlines, space news and intergalactic dog-ally jokes.

### Science Sniff Test

Science news, new research, AI claims, climate findings, health claims, materials, minerals and "is this real?" moments.

Red Dog angle: what is the claim, what is the evidence, and what are we not ready to say yet?

### Life Hack

One practical tip in words normal people can use: scams, gadgets, forms, travel, recording setup, food, repairs or everyday AI use.

Red Dog angle: what is one useful thing here?

### Onboarding

How the show works, how guests choose animals, how listeners send ideas and how people can join without feeling silly.

Red Dog angle: here is the front door.

### Merch Table

Shirts, stickers, cans, posters, digital support bundles, theme-song drops and joke products.

Red Dog angle: would this be funny, useful, supportive, or just become junk?

### Dogs And Allies

Community shout-outs, guest follow-ups, helpful people, local groups, listeners, builders, artists, sports clubs and cross-project allies.

Red Dog angle: who helped, what did they do, and what door did it open?

## Simple Rhythm Shape

Open with one bit that matches the day.

Move into the main topic.

Use Comedy Minute, Life Hack, Science Sniff Test or a dog ritual when the topic gets too dense.

Use Music Drop, Film Club, Art Show or Games Table when the episode wants a creative turn.

Close with Good Dog, Dogs And Allies or a small useful doorway forward.
