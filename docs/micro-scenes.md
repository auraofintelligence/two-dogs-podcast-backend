# Micro Scenes Pipeline

Status: working menu
Generated: 2026-05-18

## Purpose

Micro scenes are tiny animated cutaways that keep a longer conversation alive. They are not full scenes, episode arcs or recurring segments. They are quick visual bridges: a dog reaction, a source-check jump, a gate interruption, a walk, a treat, a stick, a ferry cue, then back to the yarn.

Use [builders/micro-scenes.html](../builders/micro-scenes.html) to export a compact `.md` note for each beat.

## Core Boundaries

- Angel is Blue Dog and directs that voice.
- Red Dog can set up the gag, research jump, source check or return line.
- Guests choose their own spirit animal character and nickname.
- Keep the beat short enough that the conversation still owns the show.
- Current-event or factual claims still need normal source checks.

## Pipeline Shape

Each micro scene form uses the same practical path:

1. Entry trigger: sound, prop, line, interruption or movement.
2. Three short frames: beginning, action, punchline or reveal.
3. Optional time card: useful for research jumps such as `(three minutes later)`.
4. Blue Dog space: left blank unless Angel supplies it.
5. Red Dog beat: one setup, reaction or return line.
6. Guest or ally boundary: only use chosen animal details after consent or guest choice.
7. Return to conversation: the gag hands the yarn back cleanly.
8. SFX and edit notes: one or two useful production cues.

## Starting Micro Beats

### Postman Arrives

The dogs hear a postie, parcel, scooter or gate sound and briefly lose all professional dignity.

Use when: the episode needs an interruption, excitement spike or topic reset.

### Research Time-Lapse

Red Dog starts checking a source trail, the edit flashes through notes and tabs, then a `(time length) later` card brings him back with one plain-English finding.

Use when: the show needs to admit that facts were checked, not magically known.

### Someone At The Gate

A latch, footsteps or visitor sound turns the gate into a transition point.

Use when: a guest, neighbour, delivery, location change or new topic needs a natural doorway.

### Walk And Talk

The dogs take the conversation outside so the idea can breathe.

Use when: the topic is getting dense and a moving visual will make it feel human again.

### Tree Stop

A shameless but tasteful dog-business pause on a walk.

Use when: the joke is about priorities, territory, basic needs or cutting through overthinking.

### Fetch The Stick

A stick becomes a sacred object, visual prop or metaphor.

Use when: the idea is about chasing, retrieving, testing, loyalty or returning with something useful.

### Fetch The Ball

A ball rolls through the frame and drags the energy into motion.

Use when: the point is momentum, feedback loops, training, habits or playful proof.

### Dog Treats

The treat jar wins the room.

Use when: the episode needs a reward beat, thank-you moment, support nudge or comic bribery.

### Tail Sniff Check

Dog instinct interrupts human overthinking for a quick due-diligence gag.

Use when: the conversation needs to check assumptions before trusting a story.

### Grooming Reset

Fur, collar, paws or dignity get tidied before the dogs continue.

Use when: the topic got tangled and needs a calmer restart.

### Beach Zoomies

The dogs burn off excess energy on the sand and return ready to simplify the idea.

Use when: the episode has too much excitement to stay still.

### Can Crack

A short can crack, fizz or clink marks a tonal shift.

Use when: moving into a yarn, mock sponsor, campfire moment or theme-song callback.

### Ferry Watch

A ferry, boat horn, bay crossing or water wake becomes the scene bridge.

Use when: moving from island to wider world, or from local story to bigger system.

### Guest Animal Reveal

The guest-chosen animal and nickname enter the animated world exactly as supplied by the guest.

Use when: the guest has already chosen their animal and nickname, and the reveal should support their agency.

## Next Useful Action

When a micro scene becomes a favourite, keep using the micro-scene pipeline for individual shot notes. If it grows into a named show bit, promote it into the Segment or Recurring Scene builder.
