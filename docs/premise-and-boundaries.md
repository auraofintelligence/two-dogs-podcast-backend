# Premise and Boundaries

## One-Line Premise

Two animated CGI/AI heelers host a playful, future-facing yarn show where real conversations become animal-hosted scenes, episode arcs, local set pieces and guest-chosen spirit animal appearances.

## Hosts

Red Dog is Luke / Red Heeler.

Red Dog can carry Luke's deeper context: Strange But True, Aura, P4A, personal AI, agent systems, future websites, island experiments, joyful responsible abundance and the practical work of making strange ideas usable.

Blue Dog is Angel / Blue Heeler.

Blue Dog is present as co-host, but Angel directs that voice. This repo can leave space for Blue Dog responses, beats or questions, but it must not write Blue Dog's inner voice, opinions, instructions or full scripted lines unless Angel supplies them.

## Guests

Guests choose their own spirit animal character and nickname.

The planning repo can record what the guest chooses, why they chose it, how they want to be represented and what visual boundaries matter. It must not assign the animal, nickname or symbolism on their behalf.

## Tone

Practical, playful, cheeky when it earns it, and grounded in real conversations.

The show can be strange, cosmic and future-facing, but it should still feel like a yarn on Island Country with mates trying to leave the world a bit kinder than they found it.

## Public And Source Boundaries

This workbench is public-facing.

Source notes, personal context, deep datasets, raw guest notes and early prompt experiments should still be rewritten before they become public episode copy.

## What This Repo Is

- a place to capture episode seeds
- a place to plan scenes
- a place to keep source trails
- a place to prepare agent handoffs
- a place to avoid losing good ideas

## What This Repo Is Not

- not a finished public podcast site
- not a production render pipeline
- not a legal policy system
- not a personality simulator for Angel
- not a place to assign identities to guests
